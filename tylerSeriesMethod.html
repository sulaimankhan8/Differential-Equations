Taylor Series Method



The Taylor series method is a numerical technique used to solve ordinary differential equations (ODEs). This method approximates the solution of an ODE by expanding it into a Taylor series around a point. The Taylor series method is particularly useful for solving initial value problems.


Taylor Series Method for solving ODEs


consider an initial value problem


y'(x) = f(x,y),   y(x0) = y0

The Taylor series expansion of the solution y(x) around x0 is given by:


y(x) = y(x0) + (x − x0)y'(x0) + (x-x0)2 2!y"(x0) + (x-x0)3 3!y'''(x0) + . . .

Steps to apply the Taylor Series Method


1.Calculate the Derivatives: Compute the necessary higher-order derivatives of y at x0.For example:

  • y'= f(x,y)
  • y"= d dx f(x,y) = ∂f ∂x + (∂f) (∂x) y'
  • y'"= d dx y ,and so on

2.Evaluate Derivatives at Initial Point: evaluate the derivatives at the initial point (x0,y0)

3.Construct the Taylor Series: Form the Taylor series up to the desired order ùëõ

4.Approximate the Solution: Use the Taylor series to approximate the solution at (x0+h)

Example


Consider the ODE

dy dx = x + y

with the initial condition y(0)= 1


    Differential Equation and initial values

  • Differential Equation: y' = x + y

  • Initial Condition: y(0) =1

  • Approximate at: x = 0.1

  • Taylor Series Order: 4


Compute Derivatives:


We need to compute the derivatives of y at x0 .Given y' = x + y, , we can compute higher-order derivatives as follows:


  • First Derivative y' at x=0 ,y =1:

    y'(x)= x + y

    y" = 0 + 1 = 1



  • Second Derivative y" at x =0 ,y'= 1:

    Differentiate y':

    y" = d dx (x + y) = 1 + y'
    y"(0)= 1 + 1 = 2


  • Third Derivative y''' at x =0 ,y"= 2:

    Differentiate y'':

    y''' = d dx (x + y') = 1 + y''

    y'''(0) = 2


  • Forth Derivative y'''' at x =0 ,y'''= 2:

    Differentiate y''':

    y'''' = d dx (y") = y'''

    y''''(0) = 2

The Taylor series expansion of y(x) around x0 up to order 4 is :


y(x) = y(x0) + y'(x0) 1! (x - x0) + y''(x0) 2! (x - x0)2 + y'''(x0) 3! (x - x0)3 + . . .
